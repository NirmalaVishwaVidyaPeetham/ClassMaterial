CV vs. Resume: The Ultimate Guide for Teens 🚀
==============================================

So, you're looking to get a job, apply for a cool internship, or maybe just get ready for college apps? You've probably heard adults throwing around the words **CV** and **Resume**.

At first, they sound like the same thing, but they actually have different "vibes" and uses. Let’s break it down in plain English.

1. The Quick Breakdown: What’s the Difference?

----------------------------------------------

Think of it like social media:

* **A Resume is like your TikTok or Instagram Profile.** It’s short, punchy, and shows off your best highlights right now. It's usually just 1 page.

* **A CV (Curriculum Vitae) is like your Wikipedia page.** It’s a full history of everything you’ve ever done academically and professionally. It can be 2, 3, or even 10 pages long.

| Feature     | Resume                     | CV                                            |
| ----------- | -------------------------- | --------------------------------------------- |
| **Length**  | Short (Strictly 1-2 pages) | Long (As many as needed)                      |
| **Focus**   | Skills and work experience | Academic achievements and "the whole story"   |
| **Purpose** | To get a specific job      | For research, teaching, or academic positions |

2. All About the Resume (The Job Hunter)

----------------------------------------

A resume is what you use when you want to work at a coffee shop, a retail store, or a summer camp. Managers are busy—they usually spend about **6 seconds** looking at a resume before deciding to keep it or toss it.

### Step-by-Step: How to Build a Resume

1. **Contact Info:** Name, phone number, and a _professional_ email.

2. **Summary/Objective:** 1-2 sentences about who you are.

3. **Experience:** Includes volunteering, babysitting, or lawn mowing. Use action verbs like _Managed_ or _Organized_.

4. **Education:** Your school name and expected graduation date.

5. **Skills:** Things you're good at (e.g., Photoshop, First Aid, Speaking Spanish).

### 👔 How Adults Level Up the Resume

When you're an adult with a career, your resume changes:

* **Metrics & Numbers:** Instead of just saying "I sold clothes," an adult says "Increased sales by 20% over 6 months."

* **Professional Summary:** Instead of an "Objective" (what you want), adults use a "Summary" (what they've already proven they can do).

* **Work History:** It focuses purely on relevant professional jobs, leaving off the high school babysitting or lawn mowing.
3. All About the CV (The Academic Heavyweight)

----------------------------------------------

In the US, you mostly only use a CV for specialized science programs, scholarships, or academic jobs.

### Step-by-Step: How to Build a CV

1. **Education:** Go deep. List relevant classes and your GPA.

2. **Awards & Honors:** Honor roll, sports trophies, or scouting awards.

3. **Extracurriculars:** Every club and your role in it.

4. **Projects/Research:** Science fair projects or major papers.

### 🔬 The "Pro" Sections: Publications & Research

For college students, scientists, or professors, the CV includes things you probably don't have _yet_, but might soon:

* **Publications:** If you write an article for a scientific journal, a book, or even a major school magazine, it goes here.
  
  * _Example:_ "Rivera, A. (2024). _The Impact of Solar Flares on Local WiFi._ Northview Science Journal."

* **Research Experience:** Detailed descriptions of lab work or data collection you did with a teacher or professor.

* **Conferences:** If you ever give a speech or present a poster at a big event, you list the event name and your topic.

* **Grants & Fellowships:** Any money you were given to help you study or do research.

4. Final Pro-Tips for Success 🏆

--------------------------------

* **Keep it clean:** Use a simple font like Arial or Calibri.

* **Proofread:** A typo in your phone number means they can't call you!

* **Be honest:** Don't exaggerate your skills.

* **Save as a PDF:** This ensures your formatting stays perfect on any device.

You've got this! Start with the Resume first—it's the one you'll likely use most often right now.
