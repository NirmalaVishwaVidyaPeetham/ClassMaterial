The Language of Parts: Ratios, Proportions, and Percentages
===========================================================

Fractions are the foundation for understanding how we compare quantities. Once you understand fractions, you can easily master **Ratios**, **Percentages**, and **Proportions**, as they are essentially different ways of saying the same thing.

1. Ratios: Comparing Two Quantities

-----------------------------------

A **ratio** is a comparison of two numbers by division. It tells us how much of one thing there is compared to another.

* **Fraction Connection:** A ratio $a:b$ can be written as the fraction $\frac{a}{b}$.

* **Example:** In a class, there are 12 girls and 8 boys.
  
  * The ratio of girls to boys is $12:8$.
  
  * Simplified (like a fraction), this is $3:2$.
  
  * This means for every 3 girls, there are 2 boys.
2. Percentages: Fractions of 100

--------------------------------

A **percentage** is simply a fraction where the denominator is always **100**. The word "percent" literally means "per cent" (per hundred).

* **Fraction Connection:** $x\%$ is just $\frac{x}{100}$.

* **Example:** If you get $85\%$ on a test, it means you got $\frac{85}{100}$ of the questions right.

* **Conversion:** To turn a fraction into a percentage, multiply by 100.
  
  * $\frac{3}{4} \times 100 = 75\%$
3. Proportions: Equality of Ratios

----------------------------------

A **proportion** is an equation that states that two ratios (or fractions) are equal.Formula: $\frac{a}{b} = \frac{c}{d}$

### A. Direct Proportion

In a direct proportion, as one value increases, the other increases at the same rate. Their ratio stays constant ($\frac{y}{x} = k$).

* **Real-World Example:** Buying Apples.
  
  * If 2 kg of apples cost $\$4$, then 4 kg will cost $\$8$.
  
  * As the weight **increases**, the cost **increases**.
  
  * Ratio: $\frac{4}{2} = \frac{8}{4} = 2$ (The price per kg is constant).

### B. Indirect (Inverse) Proportion

In an indirect proportion, as one value increases, the other **decreases**. Their product stays constant ($x \times y = k$).

* **Real-World Example:** Speed and Time.
  
  * If it takes 2 hours to reach a destination driving at $60\text{ km/h}$, how long does it take at $120\text{ km/h}$?
  
  * As speed **increases**, the time taken **decreases**.
  
  * Product: $60 \times 2 = 120$ and $120 \times 1 = 120$. The total distance stays the same.
4. Summary Table: How They Relate

---------------------------------

| Concept        | Representation              | Meaning                             | Example                     |
| -------------- | --------------------------- | ----------------------------------- | --------------------------- |
| **Fraction**   | $\frac{3}{4}$               | 3 parts out of 4 total.             | $\frac{3}{4}$ of a cake.    |
| **Ratio**      | $3:1$                       | 3 parts of A for every 1 part of B. | 3 cups flour : 1 cup sugar. |
| **Percentage** | $75\%$                      | 75 parts out of 100.                | $75\%$ of students passed.  |
| **Proportion** | $\frac{3}{4} = \frac{6}{8}$ | Two ratios are equivalent.          | Doubling a recipe.          |

Comprehensive Practice Exercises
--------------------------------

Test your understanding with these 20 exercises covering all topics.

### Part 1: Ratios & Percentages

1. Simplify the ratio $24:36$.

2. Write the ratio of 40 minutes to 2 hours in simplest form.

3. Convert $\frac{7}{20}$ into a percentage.

4. Convert $12.5\%$ into a fraction in simplest form.

5. In a bag of 50 marbles, 15 are red. What is the ratio of red marbles to non-red marbles?

6. If a $20\%$ discount on a shirt saves you $\$12$, what was the original price of the shirt?

### Part 2: Direct Proportion

7. If 5 shirts cost $\$75$, how much do 12 shirts cost?

8. A map scale is $1:50,000$. If two towns are $8\text{ cm}$ apart on the map, what is the actual distance in kilometers?

9. A recipe for 4 people requires $200\text{g}$ of flour. How much flour is needed for 14 people?

10. A car travels $240\text{ km}$ on $20\text{ liters}$ of petrol. How much petrol is needed for a $600\text{ km}$ trip?

11. If $y$ is directly proportional to $x$, and $y=10$ when $x=2$, find $y$ when $x=7$.

12. A shadow of a $2\text{m}$ pole is $3.5\text{m}$ long. How tall is a building that casts a $28\text{m}$ shadow at the same time?

### Part 3: Indirect (Inverse) Proportion

13. If 6 pipes can fill a tank in 80 minutes, how long will it take 5 pipes to fill the same tank?

14. A journey takes 4 hours at an average speed of $90\text{ km/h}$. How long would it take at $120\text{ km/h}$?

15. 10 workers can complete a task in 12 days. How many workers are needed to finish it in 3 days?

16. If $y$ is inversely proportional to $x$, and $y=6$ when $x=4$, find $y$ when $x=3$.

17. A bag of food lasts 5 dogs for 12 days. How long would it last for 15 dogs?

### Part 4: Mixed Challenge

18. Find $15\%$ of a ratio $4:1$ where the total quantity is 200. (Find $15\%$ of the larger part).

19. A gear with 20 teeth is meshed with a gear with 50 teeth. If the smaller gear makes 100 revolutions, how many does the larger one make? (Hint: This is inverse proportion).

20. In a school of 800 students, the ratio of boys to girls is $3:5$. What percentage of the students are girls?
