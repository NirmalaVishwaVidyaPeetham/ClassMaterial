Solving Equations with Totals and Ratios
========================================

In Part 4 of the previous guide, we encountered problems where we knew the **total amount** and the **ratio** between two parts. To solve these, we essentially solve a system of two equations.

### The Problem

Find $a$ and $b$ given:

1. **The Total:** $a + b = 800$

2. **The Ratio:** $\frac{a}{b} = \frac{3}{5}$ (or $a:b = 3:5$)

Method 1: The "Parts" Method (Easiest)
--------------------------------------

This is the most intuitive way to solve ratio problems.

1. **Find Total Parts:** Add the numbers in the ratio together.$3 + 5 = 8 \text{ total parts}$

2. **Find Value per Part:** Divide the total quantity by the total parts.$800 \div 8 = 100$

3. **Multiply:**
   
   * $a = 3 \text{ parts} \times 100 = 300$
   
   * $b = 5 \text{ parts} \times 100 = 500$

**Check:** $300 + 500 = 800$. It works!
Method 2: The Substitution Method (Algebraic)
---------------------------------------------

This method is useful for more complex equations.

1. **Isolate one variable in the ratio equation:** From $\frac{a}{b} = \frac{3}{5}$, we can say $a = \frac{3}{5}b$.

2. **Substitute into the total equation:** Substitute $(\frac{3}{5}b)$ for $a$ in $a + b = 800$:$\frac{3}{5}b + b = 800$

3. **Combine like terms:** $\frac{3}{5}b + \frac{5}{5}b = 800 \implies \frac{8}{5}b = 800$

4. **Solve for** $b$**:** $b = 800 \times \frac{5}{8} = 100 \times 5 = 500$

5. **Find** $a$**:** $a = 800 - 500 = 300$

Homework Exercises
------------------

Solve for $a$ and $b$ in the following scenarios. Show your steps using either the **Parts Method** or **Substitution**.

1. $a + b = 100$ and $a:b = 1:4$

2. $a + b = 72$ and $a:b = 5:3$

3. $a + b = 150$ and $\frac{a}{b} = \frac{2}{3}$

4. $a + b = 1,000$ and $a:b = 7:3$

5. $a + b = 45$ and $\frac{a}{b} = \frac{4}{5}$

6. **Word Problem:** The ratio of sugar to flour in a mix is $2:7$. The total weight of the mix is $450\text{g}$. How much of each is there?

7. **Word Problem:** A $120\text{cm}$ board is cut into two pieces in a ratio of $3:5$. Find the length of the shorter piece.

8. **Word Problem:** In a farm, the ratio of chickens to cows is $10:1$. There are 121 animals in total. How many chickens are there?

9. **Challenge:** $a + b = 210$ and $a$ is $25\%$ of $b$. (Hint: What is the ratio $a:b$ if $a$ is $25\%$ of $b$?)

10. **Challenge:** Three numbers $a, b,$ and $c$ add up to $180$. Their ratio is $2:3:4$. Find the value of each. (Hint: Use the Parts Method with 3 numbers).
