20 Combined Math Word Problems (Grade 1 + Grade 2)
==================================================

This collection combines various topics including fractions, decimals, exponents, GCD/LCM, Roman numerals, and measurements.
Section 1: Fractions, Decimals, and Basic Operations

----------------------------------------------------

1. **Combined Arithmetic & Decimals:** A baker makes a total of $12.5$ kilograms of dough. He uses $3\frac{1}{4}$ kg for bread and $4.85$ kg for cookies. How many kilograms of dough does he have left?
   
   * **Solution:**
     
     * Convert $3\frac{1}{4}$ to a decimal: $3 + 0.25 = 3.25$ kg.
     
     * Total used: $3.25 + 4.85 = 8.1$ kg.
     
     * Remaining dough: $12.5 - 8.1 = 4.4$ kg.
     
     * **Answer:** $4.4$ kg.

2. **Fractions & Measurement:** A construction team has completed $\frac{2}{5}$ of a $10$-mile road. If they finish another $\frac{1}{4}$ of the original road's length, how many miles do they still need to build?
   
   * **Solution:**
     
     * $\frac{2}{5}$ of $10 = \frac{2}{5} \times 10 = 4$ miles.
     
     * $\frac{1}{4}$ of $10 = \frac{1}{4} \times 10 = 2.5$ miles.
     
     * Total completed: $4 + 2.5 = 6.5$ miles.
     
     * Remaining: $10 - 6.5 = 3.5$ miles.
     
     * **Answer:** $3.5$ miles.

3. **Powers of Ten & Money:** A bank account balance is \$$123.45$. If a machine deposits $10^2$ cents and then withdraws $10^{-1}$ dollars, what is the new balance? (Note: $1$ dollar = $100$ cents).
   
   * **Solution:**
     
     * $10^2$ cents $= 100$ cents $= \$1.00$ deposit.
     
     * $10^{-1}$ dollars $= \frac{1}{10}$ dollar $= \$0.10$ withdrawal.
     
     * Calculation: $123.45 + 1.00 - 0.10 = 124.45 - 0.10 = 124.35$.
     
     * **Answer:** $\$124.35$.

4. **Decimals & Division:** An $8.75$-meter piece of fabric is cut into $5$ equal sections. What is the length of each section in meters? Express the answer as a fraction in its simplest form.
   
   * **Solution:**
     
     * Decimal division: $8.75 \div 5 = 1.75$ meters.
     
     * Convert to fraction: $1\frac{75}{100} = 1\frac{3}{4} = \frac{7}{4}$.
     
     * **Answer:** $1.75$ m or $\frac{7}{4}$ m.

5. **Mixed Numbers & Subtraction:** Mary has $5\frac{1}{3}$ cups of flour. A recipe requires $2\frac{2}{3}$ cups. If she makes the recipe twice, how much flour, in cups, will she have left?
   
   * **Solution:**
     
     * Flour needed: $2 \times 2\frac{2}{3} = 2 \times \frac{8}{3} = \frac{16}{3} = 5\frac{1}{3}$ cups.
     
     * Remaining flour: $5\frac{1}{3} - 5\frac{1}{3} = 0$.
     
     * **Answer:** $0$ cups.

Section 2: Exponents, Roots, and Roman Numerals
-----------------------------------------------

6. **Exponents & Addition:** If $4^3$ people attend a concert on Friday and $5^2$ people attend on Saturday, what is the total number of people who attended both days?
   
   * **Solution:**
     
     * $4^3 = 4 \times 4 \times 4 = 64$.
     
     * $5^2 = 5 \times 5 = 25$.
     
     * Total: $64 + 25 = 89$.
     
     * **Answer:** $89$ people.

7. **Roman Numerals & Comparison:** Convert the Roman numeral **CMXLIX** to an Indo-Arabic number. Is this result greater than or less than the total count of days in $3 \times 10^2$ years? (Assume a year has $365$ days).
   
   * **Solution:**
     
     * CMXLIX: $CM (900) + XL (40) + IX (9) = 949$.
     
     * Days in years: $3 \times 10^2 = 300$ years. $300 \times 365 = 109,500$ days.
     
     * Comparison: $949 < 109,500$.
     
     * **Answer:** $949$; it is less than the total count of days.

8. **Roots & Basic Arithmetic:** Find the cube root of $216$ = ($\sqrt[3]{216}$) and the square root of $64$ = ($\sqrt{64}$). Multiply these two results, and then subtract the difference between $12$ and $5$.
   
   * **Solution:**
     
     * $\sqrt[3]{216} = 6$ (since $6^3 = 216$).
     
     * $\sqrt{64} = 8$ (since $8^2 = 64$).
     
     * Product: $6 \times 8 = 48$.
     
     * Subtract difference: $48 - (12 - 5) = 48 - 7 = 41$.
     
     * **Answer:** $41$.

9. **Negative Exponents & Fractions:** Simplify the expression $2^{-2} + \left(\frac{1}{3}\right)^2$. Express your answer as a single fraction.
   
   * **Solution:**
     
     * $2^{-2} = \frac{1}{2^2} = \frac{1}{4}$.
     
     * $(\frac{1}{3})^2 = \frac{1}{9}$.
     
     * Addition: $\frac{1}{4} + \frac{1}{9} = \frac{9}{36} + \frac{4}{36} = \frac{13}{36}$.
     
     * **Answer:** $\frac{13}{36}$.

10. **Roman Numerals & Operations:** You see the number **LXXXIII** on a clock tower. If you subtract $18$ from this number, convert the final result back into Roman numerals.
    
    * **Solution:**
    
    * LXXXIII: $50 + 30 + 3 = 83$.
    
    * Subtraction: $83 - 18 = 65$.
    
    * Convert back: $60 (LX) + 5 (V) = LXV$.
    
    * **Answer:** LXV.

Section 3: GCD, LCM, and Divisibility Rules
-------------------------------------------

11. **GCD & Simplifying Fractions:** A fraction is $\frac{144}{216}$. First, find the Greatest Common Divisor (GCD) of the numerator and the denominator, and then use the GCD to simplify the fraction to its lowest terms.
    
    * **Solution:**
    
    * Factors of $144$: $1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 8, 9, 12, 16, 18, 24, 36, 48, 72, 144$.
    
    * Factors of $216$: $1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 8, 9, 12, 18, 24, 27, 36, 54, 72, 108, 216$.
    
    * GCD $= 72$.
    
    * Simplify: $\frac{144 \div 72}{216 \div 72} = \frac{2}{3}$.
    
    * **Answer:** GCD is $72$; simplified fraction is $\frac{2}{3}$.

12. **LCM & Fraction Addition:** Sarah is adding $\frac{5}{12}$ and $\frac{7}{18}$. Use the Least Common Multiple (LCM) of the denominators to find the Least Common Denominator (LCD). What is the sum of the two fractions using the LCD?
    
    * **Solution:**
    
    * Multiples of $12$: $12, 24, 36, 48 \dots$
    
    * Multiples of $18$: $18, 36, 54 \dots$
    
    * LCM $= 36$.
    
    * Convert: $\frac{5 \times 3}{12 \times 3} = \frac{15}{36}$ and $\frac{7 \times 2}{18 \times 2} = \frac{14}{36}$.
    
    * Sum: $\frac{15 + 14}{36} = \frac{29}{36}$.
    
    * **Answer:** $\frac{29}{36}$.

13. **Divisibility Rules & Integers:** A number is $1,234,5X0$, where $X$ is a missing digit. If the number is divisible by $9$, what is the value of the missing digit $X$?
    
    * **Solution:**
    
    * Sum of digits: $1 + 2 + 3 + 4 + 5 + X + 0 = 15 + X$.
    
    * For divisibility by $9$, the sum must be a multiple of $9$.
    
    * Possible multiples: $18, 27 \dots$
    
    * If $15 + X = 18$, then $X = 3$.
    
    * **Answer:** $3$.

14. **Last Digits of Powers & Subtraction:** What is the last digit of the final result of $7^{45} - 3^{22}$?
    
    * **Solution:**
    
    * $7^n$ cycle: $7, 9, 3, 1$ (period 4). $45 \div 4$ has remainder $1$, so last digit is $7$.
    
    * $3^n$ cycle: $3, 9, 7, 1$ (period 4). $22 \div 4$ has remainder $2$, so last digit is $9$.
    
    * Subtraction: $7 - 9 \rightarrow 17 - 9 = 8$.
    
    * **Answer:** $8$.

15. **Prime Factorization & Comparison:** Find the prime factorization of $72$ and $108$. Compare the number of distinct prime factors for each number.
    
    * **Solution:**
    
    * $72 = 8 \times 9 = 2^3 \times 3^2$. Distinct factors: $\{2, 3\}$.
    
    * $108 = 4 \times 27 = 2^2 \times 3^3$. Distinct factors: $\{2, 3\}$.
    
    * Comparison: Both have $2$ distinct prime factors.
    
    * **Answer:** Both have 2 distinct prime factors ($2$ and $3$).

16. **LCM & Scheduling:** Two buses leave the station at the same time. Bus A returns to the station every $45$ minutes, and Bus B returns every $60$ minutes. After how many minutes will they next return to the station at the same time?
    
    * **Solution:**
    
    * Find LCM of $45$ and $60$.
    
    * $45 = 3^2 \times 5$; $60 = 2^2 \times 3 \times 5$.
    
    * LCM $= 2^2 \times 3^2 \times 5 = 4 \times 9 \times 5 = 180$.
    
    * **Answer:** $180$ minutes (or 3 hours).

Section 4: Multi-Topic Integrations
-----------------------------------

17. **Decimals, Fractions, and Area:** A rectangular garden has a length of $4\frac{1}{2}$ meters and a width of $2.5$ meters. If you use $\frac{1}{3}$ of the total garden area for planting tomatoes, how many square meters are used for tomatoes? Express the final answer as a decimal.
    
    * **Solution:**
    
    * Area $= 4.5 \times 2.5 = 11.25$ m$^2$.
    
    * Tomato area $= \frac{1}{3} \times 11.25 = 3.75$ m$^2$.
    
    * **Answer:** $3.75$ m$^2$.

18. **GCD, LCM, and Exponents:** Two numbers are $a = 2^3 \times 3^2 \times 5$ and $b = 2^2 \times 3^3 \times 7$. Find the GCD and the LCM of $a$ and $b$.
    
    * **Solution:**
    
    * GCD (lowest powers): $2^2 \times 3^2 = 4 \times 9 = 36$.
    
    * LCM (highest powers): $2^3 \times 3^3 \times 5 \times 7 = 8 \times 27 \times 35 = 216 \times 35 = 7560$.
    
    * **Answer:** GCD $= 36$; LCM $= 7560$.

19. **Properties of Numbers & Decimals:** The Distributive Property states that $a \times (b + c) = (a \times b) + (a \times c)$. Show the steps to solve the problem $4.5 \times (10.2 + 9.8)$ using the distributive property, and then calculate the final answer.
    
    * **Solution:**
    
    * Step 1: $(4.5 \times 10.2) + (4.5 \times 9.8)$.
    
    * Step 2: $45.9 + 44.1 = 90$.
    
    * Alternative (Factoring back): $4.5 \times (20) = 90$.
    
    * **Answer:** $90$.

20. **Fractions, Powers of Ten, and Roman Numerals:** Calculate the value of the expression $\frac{1}{5} + 0.1 \times 10^1$. Convert the final result to a Roman numeral.
    
    * **Solution:**
    
    * Expression: $0.2 + (0.1 \times 10) = 0.2 + 1 = 1.2$.
    
    * _Note:_ Standard Roman numerals only represent integers. If rounding to $1$, the answer is I.
    
    * **Answer:** $1.2$ (Rounded to integer: I).
