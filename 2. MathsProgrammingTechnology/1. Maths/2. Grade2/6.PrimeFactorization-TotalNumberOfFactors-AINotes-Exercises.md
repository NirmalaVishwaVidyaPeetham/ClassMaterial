Finding the Total Number of Common Factors
==========================================

To find how many common factors two numbers share, we use their **Greatest Common Divisor (GCD)**. Any common factor of two numbers is simply a factor of their GCD. Therefore, the total number of common factors of two numbers is equal to the total number of factors of their GCD.

### Step-by-Step Process

We will use the example of **72** and **108**.

#### Step 1: Find the Prime Factorization of both numbers

* **72**: $72 = 2 \times 2 \times 2 \times 3 \times 3 = 2^3 \times 3^2$

* **108**: $108 = 2 \times 2 \times 3 \times 3 \times 3 = 2^2 \times 3^3$

#### Step 2: Find the GCD using Prime Factorization

To find the GCD, take the **lowest** power of each common prime base:

* Common prime **2**: Lowest power is $2^2$

* Common prime **3**: Lowest power is $3^2$

* **GCD** = $2^2 \times 3^2 = 4 \times 9 = 36$

#### Step 3: Use the Formula for the Total Number of Factors

Once you have the prime factorization of the GCD in the form $p^a \times q^b \times r^c \dots$, the formula for the total number of factors is:

**Total Factors** $= (a + 1) * (b + 1) * (c + 1) \dots$

##### Detailed Elaboration of Step 3

This formula is based on the logic of combinations. Every factor of the GCD must be built using some combination of its prime bases. The "+1" represents the option to use a prime base **zero times** (which is essentially the number 1).

* **Choice for Prime** $p$: You can use $p$ anywhere from $0$ to $a$ times (giving $a + 1$ total choices).

* **Choice for Prime** $q$: You can use $q$ anywhere from $0$ to $b$ times (giving $b + 1$ total choices).

By multiplying these choices together, we find every possible unique combination that forms a factor.

**Example A: GCD = 16 (**$2^4$**)**

* **Exponent**: $a = 4$

* **Formula**: $(4 + 1) = 5$

* **Actual Factors**: $2^0(1), 2^1(2), 2^2(4), 2^3(8), 2^4(16)$

**Example B: GCD = 12 (**$2^2 \times 3^1$**)**

* **Exponents**: $a = 2, b = 1$

* **Formula**: $(2 + 1) \times (1 + 1) = 3 \times 2 = 6$

* **Actual Factors**: $1, 2, 4, 3, 6, 12$

#### Step 4: Apply the formula to the GCD

Our GCD is $2^2 \times 3^2$.

* The exponent for base 2 is **2**.

* The exponent for base 3 is **2**.

Apply the formula:

* $(2 + 1) \times (2 + 1)$

* $3 \times 3 = 9$

**Result:** 72 and 108 have exactly **9 common factors**.

### Verification

The common factors of 72 and 108 are the factors of 36: **1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 9, 12, 18, 36** (Total: 9)

### Summary Formula

If $GCD(x, y) = p_1^{a_1} \times p_2^{a_2} \times \dots \times p_n^{a_n}$

Total Common Factors = $(a_1 + 1)(a_2 + 1) \dots (a_n + 1)$
